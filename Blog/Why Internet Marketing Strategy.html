Why an Internet Marketing Strategy is Important for your Business in 2017

Consumers trust online reviews and sources more than ever when making purchasing decisions

Posted by Kody C. Kendall on October 7, 2017

Why an Internet Marketing Strategy is Important for your Business in 2017

In 2000, Google had been around for only two years. Started in the dorm room of two graduate students in 1998, Google has now grown to be the 2nd largest company in the world, being just 1% smaller than the 1st largest company, Apple.

The top five largest companies in the world are all internet/computer companies.

  1. Apple
  2. Google (Also known as Alphabet)
  3. Microsoft
  4. Amazon
  5. Facebook

The internet has taken over the economy and world of business like no other technology ever has.

Despite the fact that only 60% of United States citizens owned a personal computer in 2000, there were roughly 14 billion internet searches on Google annually.  That’s roughly 50 Google searches per person in the United States in 2000.

In 2017, it was estimated that there was more than two trillion Google searches a year, while over 85% of the United States population now owns a personal computer,  88% frequently use the internet, 77% own a smart-phone that has access to the internet, and 69% use Social Media frequently.

http://www.pewresearch.org/fact-tank/2017/01/12/evolution-of-technology/

Combine the internet’s massive growth with the fact that an estimated 88% of customers trust online reviews from their peers just as much as a personal recommendations from somebody they know.

https://searchengineland.com/88-consumers-trust-online-reviews-much-personal-recommendations-195803

It’s well known that word-of-mouth marketing pays massive dividends in bringing in new customers and retaining the old ones. But if 88% of consumers rely on online reviews and recommendations just as much as personal recommendations, businesses that don’t have an online presence are missing out on massive growth potential that can come from having active social-media accounts and having a well-designed, professional, and practical website.

This means that the online reputation for a business — especially for a small, local business — can be one of the most important tools for high customer growth as well as customer retention.

People trust businesses that are recommended by their peers, that have a good reputation and online presence.

The internet’s growth and people’s reliance on search-engine’s like Google to find local businesses combined with the fact that customers put so much faith in online reviews means that building an online presence has never been a smarter move for a growing business.
