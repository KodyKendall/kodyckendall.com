About the blog

According to Pew Research Center, 26% of American adults say that they haven't read -- even just parts -- of a book in the last year. That's more than 1 in 4 Americans.

Only 34% of Americans can name all three branches of the federal government, and only 37% can name their congressional representatives. How can the people of a country be empowered if they don't even understand the basic functionings of their government?

Reading and writing are important. Reading is how we come to learn from people's past mistakes, and how we come to grow and learn as individuals and as a society, while writing is how information is shared and refined. It's much easier to critique bad ideas when they're written down, which is why writing frequently can improve criticla thinking skills.

Even though I'm only 21 years old, I try to read and write as much as possible to learn from those who have gone before me, and to pass on anything I learn along the way. I'm the true recipient of benefits from this blog, but If I can pass on even a little bit of knowledge, wisdom, or appreciation to someone along the way, that's good too.
